# GREED Academy Public Research

Date: 2026-04-02 KST

Purpose:

- collect publicly verifiable GREED Academy metadata
- identify what appears to have worked well
- translate those signals into a landing-page infographic for OnChain Korea

## Scope

This note uses only public sources that were accessible at the time of research.  
It does **not** claim private internal performance, confidential enrollment data, or undisclosed learner satisfaction figures.

## Publicly verifiable metadata

### Course library structure

- Learn hub exposes:
  - Semester 1: 8 lessons
  - Semester 2: 8 lessons
  - Tokens course: 7 lessons
  - DeFi course: 6 lessons
- Total public source units visible from the hub: 29

Source:
- https://greed.academy/learn

### Semester 1 public results

The official Semester 1 about page states:

- 4,000+ unique learners
- 2,700+ completed lessons
- 1,000+ stakers locked for the full journey
- $100,000+ in rewards distributed

The same page also says students kept coming back after rewards were claimed, and frames the successful ingredients as structured, interactive, and rewarding.

Source:
- https://greed.academy/learn/semesters/1/about

### Semester 2 public mechanics

The official Semester 2 about page states:

- reward pool: $120,000+
- 8 lessons
- at least 4 of 8 lessons required to qualify
- 90-day SOL lock
- real-time leaderboard
- lock without unstaking is supported

Key date window shown publicly:

- Semester 2 runs from April 14 to July 7, 2025
- lessons start on May 14, 2025

Source:
- https://greed.academy/learn/semesters/2/about

## Public field signals

### Athens beginner onboarding

A public GREED Academy recap shared via Reddit, quoting GREED Academy's X thread, states:

- a 5-hour Blockchain 101 course was run in Athens
- 93 first-time users were introduced to crypto
- the course covered wallets, first onchain actions, swapping, and staking

This is a strong signal that the material can work in a live beginner setting, not just as self-serve content.

Source:
- https://www.reddit.com/r/solana/comments/1ih3kya

### Chicago workshop

The public OnionDAO Luma page describes a GREED Academy beginner workshop in Chicago. It emphasizes:

- first wallet setup
- first onchain transactions
- key crypto concepts
- daily-life use cases
- learn-by-doing, not lecture

This is useful because it shows the product is being positioned as hands-on onboarding, not content consumption alone.

Source:
- https://luma.com/0ewjdizi

### Content reuse in Vietnam community education

Multiple public Luma pages for "Web3 Starter Pack" in Hanoi say:

- the program is 8 weeks long
- lessons were compiled by GREED Academy
- mentors came from Solana ecosystem companies such as Solflare, Raydium, and Metaplex
- the program reused the GREED reward pool and education framing

This suggests the GREED curriculum is portable enough for community-led localization and offline/online hybrid teaching.

## What appears to have worked

The following are inferences from the sources above.

### 1. Structure mattered more than pure incentives

Inference:

- Semester 1 official copy says people kept returning after rewards were claimed.
- That suggests the content flow and completion framing had standalone value beyond token incentives.

### 2. Beginner onboarding was practical, not abstract

Inference:

- Athens, Chicago, and Hanoi materials repeatedly center wallet setup, first transactions, swaps, staking, and explorers.
- The winning pattern appears to be "do one real thing immediately" rather than "watch theory first."

### 3. Visible progression helped motivation

Inference:

- Leaderboards, stars, completion thresholds, and public eligibility rules appear repeatedly.
- Even if OnChain Korea should not copy the learn-to-earn structure directly, it should preserve visible progress logic.

### 4. The curriculum was reusable across communities

Inference:

- Public workshops and third-party community programs reused GREED-authored lessons.
- That suggests the source material is coherent enough to adapt into a localized 8-week Korean flow.

## Implications for OnChain Korea landing

Recommended infographic direction:

- use public facts, not fake testimonials
- avoid "students loved it" language unless it is directly sourced
- highlight:
  - 29-source-unit library
  - 4,000+ learners
  - 2,700+ completed lessons
  - $100k+ Semester 1 rewards / $120k+ Semester 2 pool
  - 93 first-time users in Athens live onboarding
  - GREED-authored content reused in community education programs

Recommended copy angle:

- "우리가 이 구조를 선택한 이유"
- "공개적으로 검증된 학습 구조를 한국형 8주 프로그램으로 재구성"

Recommended design angle:

- one proof section between "Why" and "Curriculum"
- metrics first
- then source-map infographic (8 + 8 + 7 + 6)
- then qualitative reasons this model translated well
